## üéØ About Crossover

### Q: What is Crossover?
**A:** Crossover is a global talent marketplace that connects companies with remote software engineers worldwide. They're known for competitive salaries, 100% remote work, and a highly selective hiring process.

### Q: What's the typical salary?
**A:** Salaries range from $30,000 to $150,000+ USD annually, depending on:
- Position level (Junior, Mid, Senior)
- Role type (Frontend, Backend, Full-Stack, DevOps)
- Your location (cost of living adjustments)
- Your performance in assessments

### Q: Is Crossover only for certain countries?
**A:** No! Crossover hires globally. You can work from anywhere with a reliable internet connection. However, some positions may have timezone requirements.

### Q: Do I need a computer science degree?
**A:** No. Crossover evaluates based on skills and assessment performance, not credentials. Self-taught developers, bootcamp graduates, and career changers are all welcome.

### Q: What programming languages does Crossover require?
**A:** It varies by position. Common requirements:
- **Most positions:** JavaScript, Python, Java, or C#
- **Frontend:** JavaScript/TypeScript, React, Vue, or Angular
- **Backend:** Python, Java, Node.js, or Go
- **Full-Stack:** Combination of above

The learning plan focuses on fundamentals that work for any language.

### Q: What if English isn't my first language?

## üßÆ About the CCAT Test

### Q: What is the CCAT?
**A:** The Criteria Cognitive Aptitude Test (CCAT) measures your ability to solve problems, learn new information, and think critically. It's 50 questions in 15 minutes covering:
- Math & Logic (33%)
- Verbal Reasoning (33%)
- Spatial Reasoning (33%)

### Q: What score do I need?
**A:** You need approximately:
- **Minimum:** 85th percentile (35-36/50)
- **Competitive:** 90th percentile (38-40/50)
- **Average:** 50th percentile (24/50)

Most candidates underestimate this test and get eliminated here.

### Q: Can I use a calculator?
**A:** No. Mental math skills are essential. Practice daily at https://arithmetic.zetamac.com/

### Q: How much should I prepare for CCAT?
**A:** At least 2-4 weeks of intensive practice:
- Take 10+ full practice tests
- Score consistently 35+ before applying
- Practice speed (18 seconds per question average)

### Q: Where can I practice CCAT for free?
**A:** See the learning plan for 7+ free practice tests. Start with:
- https://www.jobtestprep.com/free-ccat-practice-test
- https://www.12minprep.com/
- https://www.test-guide.com/ccat-practice-tests.html

## üíª About Technical Preparation

### Q: How many coding problems should I solve?
**A:** Minimum recommendations:
- **Basic:** 100+ problems (mostly easy)
- **Competitive:** 150+ problems (mix of easy, medium, some hard)
- **Strong:** 200+ problems (emphasize medium/hard)

Quality matters more than quantity‚Äîunderstand patterns, don't memorize solutions.

### Q: Which platform is best: LeetCode or HackerRank?
**A:** Use both!
- **HackerRank:** Mirrors Crossover's assessment style
- **LeetCode:** Better for interview patterns and discussions

The learning plan recommends specific problems from both.

### Q: Do I need to learn system design?
**A:** Basic system design is helpful for:
- Senior positions (required)
- Project evaluation (demonstrates thinking)
- Interviews (may be discussed)

For junior roles, focus more on coding fundamentals.

### Q: Should I specialize in frontend, backend, or full-stack?
**A:** Start with fundamentals (works for all). Then:
- Check current Crossover job postings
- Specialize based on your interests
- Full-stack has most opportunities

### Q: Do I need to build projects?
**A:** YES! Projects demonstrate:
- Practical coding ability
- Code organization skills
- End-to-end thinking
- GitHub activity

Aim for 6-10 complete projects in your portfolio.

## üéì Study Strategies

### Q: How many hours per day should I study?
**A:** Recommended:
- **Minimum effective:** 3-4 hours/day
- **Standard pace:** 5-6 hours/day
- **Intensive:** 8+ hours/day

Quality focused time beats endless distracted hours.

### Q: Should I study every day?
**A:** Almost! Recommended schedule:
- **Study days:** 6 days/week
- **Rest days:** 1 day/week (Sunday suggested)
- **Light days:** Occasional 2-hour days for recovery

Consistency matters more than perfection.

### Q: When's the best time to study?
**A:** Whatever works for your life! Tips:
- Study at the same time daily (builds habit)
- Do hardest work when most alert
- Morning often best for problem-solving
- Evening good for review/easier tasks

### Q: How do I stay motivated for months?
**A:** Strategies that work:
- Track progress visibly (use the spreadsheet!)
- Join study communities
- Share progress on social media
- Find an accountability partner
- Remember your "why"
- Celebrate small wins weekly
- Take scheduled breaks

### Q: What if I don't understand something?
**A:** Normal! Try:
1. Watch 2-3 different explanations (YouTube)
2. Read multiple articles on the topic
3. Ask in coding communities (Reddit, Discord, Stack Overflow)
4. Solve easier problems first, come back later
5. Explain it to someone else (or rubber duck)

Confusion is part of learning.

## üö´ Common Mistakes

### Q: What's the biggest mistake applicants make?
**A:** Applying too early! Many people:
- Apply after 1 month of casual study
- Score 28/50 on CCAT (thinking it's "good enough")
- Have weak English
- Haven't practiced under time pressure

Result: Rejected, must wait months to reapply.

**Better:** Wait until truly ready, then ace it first try.

### Q: What else do people get wrong?
**A:** Common pitfalls:
- Skipping CCAT preparation completely
- Only watching tutorials, not coding
- Memorizing solutions vs understanding patterns
- Not practicing with time limits
- Submitting incomplete/messy project
- Poor code quality (unreadable, no comments)
- Not testing code thoroughly

### Q: Is tutorial hell real?
**A:** YES! Signs you're in tutorial hell:
- Watching tons of videos but not coding
- Following along but can't code independently
- Constantly starting new courses
- Months passing without building projects

**Solution:** 80% doing (coding), 20% learning (watching).

## üîß Technical Questions

### Q: What computer do I need?
**A:** Minimum requirements:
- Modern laptop/desktop (last 5 years)
- 8GB RAM minimum (16GB better)
- Stable internet (25+ Mbps recommended)
- Webcam and microphone (for interviews)

Most mid-range laptops work fine.

### Q: Can I use any operating system?
**A:** Yes! Windows, macOS, or Linux all work. However:
- Many developers prefer macOS or Linux
- Windows users should consider WSL (Windows Subsystem for Linux)
- Choose what you're comfortable with

### Q: Do I need paid tools or software?
**A:** No! Everything needed is free:
- Code editors (VS Code, Sublime)
- Languages (Python, JavaScript, etc.)
- Practice platforms (free tiers)
- CCAT practice tests (free options available)
- GitHub (free for public repos)
